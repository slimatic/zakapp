## Creating Your First Snapshot

A **snapshot** is a record of your financial situation at a specific point in time (usually your Zakat anniversary date). Snapshots can be in **draft** or **finalized** status.

### Step 1: Start a New Snapshot

1. Click **"Create Snapshot"** button on the dashboard
2. The Create Snapshot form will appear

### Step 2: Enter Basic Information

**Snapshot Date (Gregorian):**
- Select the date for this snapshot (typically your Zakat anniversary)
- Example: January 1, 2024

**Snapshot Date (Hijri):**
- The Hijri date is auto-calculated based on the Gregorian date
- Example: Jumada al-Thani 19, 1446 AH
- You can manually adjust if needed

**Calculation Methodology:**
- Select your preferred method:
  - **Standard (2.5%)** - Most common
  - **Hanafi** - Silver nisab
  - **Shafi'i** - Gold nisab
  - **Maliki** - Gold nisab
  - **Hanbali** - Gold nisab

### Step 3: Enter Financial Data

**Total Wealth:**
- Enter the sum of all your zakatable assets
- Include: cash, bank accounts, investments, gold, silver, business inventory, trade goods
- Example: $150,000

**Total Liabilities:**
- Enter any debts or liabilities due within the year
- Example: $20,000

**Zakatable Wealth:**
- Auto-calculated: Total Wealth - Total Liabilities
- Example: $150,000 - $20,000 = $130,000

**Zakat Due:**
- Auto-calculated based on methodology (typically 2.5%)
- Example: $130,000 Ã— 2.5% = $3,250

**Nisab Threshold:**
- Current nisab value based on gold or silver price
- Example: $85,000 (gold-based)
- Updated automatically from market data

### Step 4: Add Asset Breakdown (Optional)

Break down your wealth by category for better tracking:

- **Cash:** Physical currency
- **Bank Accounts:** Checking, savings, money market
- **Investments:** Stocks, bonds, mutual funds, crypto
- **Gold:** Gold jewelry, coins, bars (zakatable amount)
- **Silver:** Silver items (zakatable amount)
- **Real Estate:** Investment properties (trade value only)
- **Business Assets:** Inventory, trade goods
- **Other:** Any other zakatable assets

### Step 5: Add Notes (Optional)

Use the notes field to:
- Record special circumstances
- Note major financial changes
- Mention methodology considerations
- Add reminders for next year

Example:
```
"Included Q4 bonus in calculations. Sold rental property 
in March. Using gold nisab due to lower silver prices."
```

## Understanding Islamic Compliance

ZakApp follows Islamic principles in all tracking features.

### Zakat Calculation Methodologies

**Standard (2.5%):**
- Most widely used
- Based on 1/40th (2.5%) of zakatable wealth
- Applies to cash, gold, silver, trade goods
- Nisab: 85 grams of gold OR 595 grams of silver

**Hanafi School:**
- Similar to standard but different nisab preference
- Often uses silver nisab (lower threshold, more inclusive)
- Specific rulings on mixed assets
- Emphasizes helping more people

**Shafi'i School:**
- Gold nisab typically used
- Higher threshold than silver
- Specific rulings on debts and liabilities
- Stricter criteria for zakatable assets

**Maliki School:**
- Gold nisab preference
- Similar to Shafi'i in many aspects
- Emphasis on asset ownership duration
- Specific rulings on business inventory

**Hanbali School:**
- Gold nisab commonly used
- Conservative approach to zakatable assets
- Specific rulings on agricultural produce
- Emphasis on precision in calculations

**Choosing Your Methodology:**
- Consult with a qualified Islamic scholar
- Consider your local community's practice
- Stick with one methodology for consistency
- ZakApp supports all major methodologies

### Nisab Threshold

**What is Nisab?**
- Minimum wealth required to obligate Zakat
- Two types: gold-based and silver-based
- Wealth must exceed nisab for full lunar year

**Gold Nisab:**
- 85 grams (3 ounces) of gold
- Approximately $6,000-$8,000 USD (varies with gold price)
- Higher threshold than silver

**Silver Nisab:**
- 595 grams (21 ounces) of silver
- Approximately $500-$700 USD (varies with silver price)
- Lower threshold (benefits more people)

**Which Nisab to Use?**
- Gold nisab: More conservative, less Zakat payers
- Silver nisab: More inclusive, helps more people
- Modern scholars often recommend silver nisab
- ZakApp lets you choose based on your preference

**ZakApp Nisab Updates:**
- Nisab values updated daily from market data
- Historical nisab values preserved in snapshots
- Alerts if your wealth crosses nisab threshold

### Lunar vs. Solar Calendar

**Hijri (Lunar) Calendar:**
- Islamic calendar used for religious obligations
- 354-355 days per year (11 days shorter than solar)
- Zakat anniversary typically calculated using Hijri

**Gregorian (Solar) Calendar:**
- Western calendar, 365-366 days
- Used for practical date tracking

**ZakApp Dual Calendar:**
- Both calendars displayed throughout
- Hijri date auto-calculated from Gregorian
- Manual adjustment available if needed
- Anniversary reminders use Hijri dates

**Zakat Due Date:**
- Obligation begins after wealth exceeds nisab for full Hijri year
- Pay Zakat as soon as obligation confirmed
- Delaying payment without valid reason is discouraged
- ZakApp tracks both Gregorian and Hijri anniversaries

## FAQ

### General Questions

**Q: What is a snapshot?**  
A: A snapshot is a record of your financial situation at a specific point in time, typically your Zakat anniversary. It captures total wealth, liabilities, Zakat due, and asset breakdown.

**Q: Can I have multiple snapshots per year?**  
A: You can create multiple drafts, but typically only one finalized snapshot per year (on your Zakat anniversary).

**Q: What's the difference between draft and finalized?**  
A: Draft snapshots can be edited and deleted. Finalized snapshots are immutable, enable payment tracking, and trigger reminders.

**Q: Can I edit a finalized snapshot?**  
A: No, finalized snapshots are immutable to maintain data integrity. Create a new snapshot if needed.

**Q: Is my data private?**  
A: Yes! All financial data is encrypted end-to-end. ZakApp never sells or shares your data.

### Calculation Questions

**Q: Which methodology should I use?**  
A: Consult with an Islamic scholar familiar with your situation. Most users choose Standard (2.5%) or follow their local community's practice.

**Q: Should I use gold or silver nisab?**  
A: Silver nisab is more inclusive (lower threshold, helps more people). Gold nisab is more conservative. Many scholars recommend silver. Choose based on your preference or consult a scholar.

**Q: How do I calculate zakatable wealth?**  
A: Zakatable Wealth = Total Zakatable Assets - Liabilities. ZakApp auto-calculates this for you.

**Q: Are cryptocurrencies zakatable?**  
A: Most scholars treat cryptocurrency as currency, making it zakatable. Include current market value in your calculations.

**Q: Do I pay Zakat on my house?**  
A: Your primary residence is not zakatable. Investment properties held for trade are zakatable based on current market value, not rental income.

### Payment Questions

**Q: How should I distribute Zakat among the 8 categories?**  
A: There's flexibility in distribution. Consider local needs and consult scholars. ZakApp tracks distribution to help you maintain balance.

**Q: Can I pay Zakat to family members?**  
A: Yes, to extended family members (not parents, children, or spouse). They must fit one of the 8 eligible categories.

**Q: Can I pay Zakat in advance?**  
A: Yes, paying in advance is permissible. ZakApp lets you record payments even before Zakat becomes obligatory.

**Q: What if I overpay Zakat?**  
A: Excess payments are considered voluntary charity (sadaqah). ZakApp warns you if payments exceed Zakat due.

**Q: Can I edit payment records?**  
A: Yes, you can edit or delete payment records anytime to correct errors.

### Technical Questions

**Q: Can I access ZakApp offline?**  
A: Basic functionality works offline. Sync occurs automatically when you reconnect.

**Q: Can I export my data?**  
A: Yes! Export snapshots, summaries, and comparison reports as PDFs anytime.

**Q: Is there a mobile app?**  
A: Currently web-based (mobile-responsive). Native mobile apps coming soon!

**Q: Can I import data from other tools?**
